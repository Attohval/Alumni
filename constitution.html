Constitution

Delta Careers College Warri Alumni 2000 Set

[As Amended]

Preamble

WHEREAS, we the Alumni of Delta Careers College, Warri having firmly and unanimously resolved to organize ourselves into a united and purposeful body to foster unity, promote the spirit of co-operation, understanding, general welfare of members and to contribute our quota to the growth and development of our alma mater, give unto ourselves the following constitution.

Articles

Title: This constitution shall be known as the Constitution of Delta Careers College Alumni, 2000.

Name: The Association shall be known as Delta Careers College Alumni, 2000 (DCC2000 ALUMNI).

Aims & Objectives:

  • To foster unity and love among members.
  • To create a social environment for interaction and integration.
  • To identify with the needs of members and the college.
  • To seek the progress and development of the alma mater.

Membership

  • Membership shall be open to anyone who spent at least one academic session in the set, subject to screening by executives.

MEMBERSHIP DUES AND LEVIES

  • All members shall be required to pay a monthly membership due and levies as may be imposed, which shall be fixed from time to time by the general house.
  • No One shall be exempted from paying dues for any reason. However, if any member feel he or she should be exempted from paying dues, he/ she has to apply to the welfare committee, stating why he or she should be exempted and for how long (Exemption is subject to approval of the committee and the Excos, after due investigation).

TERMINATION OF MEMBERSHIP

Membership shall be terminated by any of the following:-

  • Death of member.
  • Through expulsion of member.
  • Through withdrawal of membership, which shall be entertained only after 3 meetings of the Association; provided no refunds shall be made on levies already paid.

SANCTION OF MEMBERS

Members shall be sanctioned by way of fines, suspension and expulsion for any of the following offences:-

  • Repeated failure to attend meetings or contribute in the discussions via WhatsApp Group for Two (2) consecutive times without notice.
  • Failure to pay prescribed dues/levies for one year, after a review by the executive.
  • If found guilty of consistent gross misconduct on more than two occasions.

WELFARE OF MEMBERS

  • Subject to the provisions of sections 1.3 of Article 1 & 4.0 of Article 4 of this constitution, a member shall:
    • Be honoured with a delegation and the presentation of a purse by the Association at the burial ceremony of any of his/her parents; provided a purse shall not be presented on more than one occasion.
    • Be honoured with a delegation and the presentation of a purse by the Association when getting married, celebrating marriage anniversary or any social event nominated by the member, provided a purse shall be presented in not more than one occasion.
    • In both cases, notice of a reasonable time is given to the Association.
  • In the event of the death of a member not in default of provisions of section 1.4 of Article 1 of this constitution shall be entitled to a fully participated burial/funeral ceremony by all members.
  • The dependants of a departed member shall be presented a purse in the presence of the family during a condolence visit prior to the burial, of an amount to be determined by the Association.
  • Any member who fails to attend the burial/funeral ceremony of a member without an excuse, shall be liable to a fine of an amount to be determined by the house.

Organs:

  • National Executive Council
  • Board of Trustees
  • Standing Committees

National Executive Council: Elected every 2 years, with offices including President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, Welfare Officer, Auditor, etc.

Powers: Manage affairs, summon meetings, present reports, organize events, appoint committees, formulate policy.

Functions of Officers: Each office has defined duties (President, VP, Secretary, Treasurer, Financial Sec, Welfare Officer, Legal Adviser, Auditor, PRO, Chapter Chairs).

Tenure: Two years, renewable once; extensions possible if no replacements.

Board of Trustees: Nine senior members, tenure 3 years, arbitrate disputes, safeguard heritage, promote alma mater’s development.

Meetings:

  • General meeting 3 times yearly.
  • Emergency meetings by President or 2/3 Excos.
  • Branches may hold independent meetings.
  • End of year party annually.

Quorum: 8 registered/financial members.

Subject to the provision of the constitution, the General House shall have powers to make rules, regulations and sanctions to regulate its activities generally.

Nomination: Must have good attendance, dues paid, and not impeached before.

Qualifications: Punctual, active members with voting rights.

Procedure:

  • Open ballot system.
  • Election committee conducts elections.
  • Simple majority wins.
  • Fresh elections for ties.
  • Officers may seek re-election for one additional term.

Bye Election: If an officer resigns, dies, or is removed.

An officer of the Association shall be removed from office if:

  • He/she is no longer capable of exercising or performing the functions of his office.
  • He/she is found guilty of an offence of very grave nature.
  • He is consistently absent for a considerable number of meetings without cogent reasons.
  • He/she consistently refuses to behave in proper manners always, whether within or outside the Association.
  • Before an officer is removed from office, he/she must be adequately and sufficiently heard or tried and found liable.
  • An officer or member who refuses to be tried or refuses to defend himself shall be deemed to have committed the offence for which he is accused and so be accordingly treated.

Finance Sources: Monthly dues, donations, levies, fines.

Welfare:

  • Financial emergency support (subject to committee approval).
  • Dues-paying members entitled to benefits for weddings, childbirth, or parent burial.
  • Freewill donations only for dues-paying members.

Withdrawals:

  • Below ₦100k → President approval.
  • Up to ₦200k → Exco approval.
  • Above ₦200k → House approval (emergency exceptions allowed).

Imprest Account: Treasurer keeps ₦10k imprest at a time.

This constitution shall be subject to amendment from time to time according to the following procedure:

  • Any financial member of the Association shall be qualified to move a motion for the amendment of any aspect of the constitution; provided the proposal forms part of the Notice published through a circular of meeting of the Association.
  • Notice of the amendment shall be given at least a month before the date fixed for the meeting.
  • The motion for the amendment shall be supported by at least two third of the members present at a meeting properly convened.
