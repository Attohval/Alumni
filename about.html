About Our Alumni

DCCW ALUMNI 2000 Association is a vibrant network of graduates, united by shared experiences and a passion for lifelong learning, leadership, and service. We celebrate our diverse journeys and empower each other to make a difference in our communities and professions.

Our Mission

To connect, inspire, and support alumni in their personal and professional growth, while giving back to our alma mater and society.

Core Values

  • Mentorship & Collaboration
  • Celebrating Achievements
  • Community Service
  • Inclusivity & Diversity

Aims & Objectives

  • To foster unity and love among members.
  • To create social environment for interaction and integration.
  • To identify with the needs of members at all times; including Delta Careers College, Warri.
  • To seek the progress and development of our alma mater, Delta Careers College, Warri.

Our Story

​​Delta Careers College Warri's Class of 2000 is a vibrant community built on shared memories and a commitment to lifelong friendship. Though our graduation scattered us across the globe, the desire to reconnect brought us back together. ​Our first attempt to formally integrate as an Alumni was in March 2008. However, keeping the close bond alive proved challenging in the years that followed. Everything changed in August 2016—a pivotal moment when a small, dedicated group found a definitive path to forge a formidable, unbreakable bond. ​Since then, the DCC 2000 Set has been waxing incredibly strong. We are a community of like minds dedicated to bonding, networking, and mutual support. We celebrate one another, reach out in times of need, and stand together through both good times and bad. ​More than just a social group, we are driven by a spirit of giving back. We actively collaborate with the school management and have embarked on remarkable projects to support our beloved alma mater. The DCC 2000 Set is not just an alumni group — we are a family, committed to celebrating our past, supporting our present, and building a brighter future together.
